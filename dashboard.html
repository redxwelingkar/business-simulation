Total Addresable Market (TAM)

The total addressable market (TAM) refers to the total revenue opportunity available for a product or service within a specific market or industry. It represents the maximum potential sales volume that a product or service could achieve if every possible customer in the market purchased it. TAM is often used by businesses to assess the potential size and attractiveness of a market before entering or investing in it. It helps companies understand the revenue potential and make strategic decisions regarding product development, marketing, and expansion.

Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)

The serviceable addressable market (SAM) refers to the portion of the total market that a product or service provider can realistically target and serve. It represents the subset of the total market that fits within the company's capabilities, resources, and strategic focus. Identifying the SAM helps companies understand the potential size of their target market and develop effective marketing and sales strategies.

Customer spending power

Customer spending power refers to the financial capacity of consumers to purchase goods and services.It's influenced by factors like income, employment, inflation, and savings. Understanding spending power helps businesses gauge market demand and tailor their offerings accordingly.

Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)

Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) refers to the portion of the total addressable market (TAM) that a company can realistically capture, considering factors like competition, resources, and market dynamics. It represents the feasible market share a company can achieve within its target market segment.
